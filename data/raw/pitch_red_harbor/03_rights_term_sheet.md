# Rights Term Sheet: Red Harbor Collection

## Parties
- **Licensor:** Red Harbor Distribution, Ltd. (RHD)
- **Licensee:** The Platform (Decision-Support Application Owner)

## Rights Granted
1. **Non-Exclusive Streaming Rights:** To exhibit, transmit, and perform the 52 titles in the Red Harbor Collection on the Licensee's OTT platform.
2. **Derivative Rights:** Licensee has the right to develop, produce, and exhibit spin-offs, prequels, and sequels for selected titles (e.g., "Red Harbor"), subject to RHD's prior written consent.
3. **Merchandising Rights:** Rights to create and sell branded products related to the titles, subject to RHD's approval.
4. **Localization Rights:** Rights to dub and subtitle the titles for all licensed territories.

## Territories
- **Licensed Territories:** Worldwide (Excluding China, for which separate negotiations are required).
- **Territory Restrictions:** Depiction of political or societal themes in several titles may require specific cultural review/edits for distribution in certain MENA and SEA regions.

## Term Length
- **Initial Term:** 3 years from the date of the first title's release on the Platform.
- **Renewal Option:** Licensee has the right to renew for an additional 2-year term, subject to a 5% increase in the license fee.

## Exclusivity
- **Exclusive Window:** Worldwide exclusivity for the Initial Term. No other OTT or linear distribution permitted during this period for most titles.
- **Non-Exclusive Window:** RHD retains rights for certain older titles (pre-2021) to be licensed to third-party platforms.
- **Carve-outs:** RHD retains rights for educational/museum exhibition and film festival screenings.

## Termination Conditions
- **Licensee Default:** Failure to pay license fees or breach of non-compete clauses.
- **Licensor Default:** Failure to deliver high-quality master files or breach of representations and warranties.
- **Material Change:** Significant changes to the catalog mix (e.g., removal of more than 5 key titles) may trigger a termination review.

## Key Clauses for Review
- **9.2 Exclusivity Overlap:** Several key titles in the catalog (e.g., "The Berlin Cipher") have complex, overlapping exclusivity windows with other platforms.
- **5.1 Sub-Licensing Limits:** Licensee is prohibited from sub-licensing the Content to third-party platforms in specific Tier 2 regions (e.g., India, Brazil).

## Conclusion
The proposed rights deal for the Red Harbor Collection is volume-focused but includes several key risks. The exclusivity overlap and sub-licensing limitations are of particular concern for the Platform's long-term ROI and international strategy.
