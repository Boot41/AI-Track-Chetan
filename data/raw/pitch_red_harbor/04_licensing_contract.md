# Licensing Contract: Red Harbor Collection

## 1. DEFINITIONS
- **"Catalog":** The 52 titles listed in the Red Harbor Collection.
- **"Platform":** The Licensee's OTT streaming service.
- **"Territory":** Worldwide (Excluding China, for which separate negotiations are required).
- **"Initial Term":** 3 years from the date of the first title's release.
- **"RHD":** Red Harbor Distribution, Ltd.

## 2. GRANT OF RIGHTS
RHD hereby grants to the Licensee the non-exclusive right to exhibit, transmit, and perform the Catalog on the Platform throughout the Territory during the Initial Term. This includes the right to:
- **2.1. Dubbing and Subtitling:** Create and distribute localized versions of the Catalog in all languages of the Territory.
- **2.2. Merchandising:** Develop and sell physical and digital products related to selected titles, subject to Section 10.3.
- **2.3. Derivative Works:** Develop, produce, and exhibit spin-offs, prequels, and sequels for selected titles (e.g., "Red Harbor"), subject to Section 14.3.

## 3. TERRITORY RESTRICTIONS
- **3.1. Cultural Review:** Licensee acknowledges that the depiction of political or societal themes in several titles (e.g., "The Spanish Connection") may require specific cultural review and potential edits for distribution in certain MENA and SEA regions. 
- **3.2. Compliance:** Licensee is responsible for ensuring the Catalog complies with all local laws and regulations in the Territory.

## 4. EXCLUSIVITY WINDOWS
- **4.1. Global Exclusivity:** Worldwide exclusivity for the Initial Term for most titles. No other OTT or linear distribution permitted during this period.
- **4.2. Non-Exclusive Titles:** RHD retains rights for certain older titles (pre-2021) to be licensed to third-party platforms.
- **4.3. Carve-outs:** RHD retains rights for educational/museum exhibition and film festival screenings.

## 5. SUB-LICENSING PROVISIONS
- **5.1. Limited Sub-Licensing:** Licensee is prohibited from sub-licensing the Content to third-party platforms in specific Tier 2 regions (e.g., India, Brazil). 
- **5.2. Prior Written Consent:** For all other regions, Licensee may sub-license the Catalog with RHD's prior written consent, which shall not be unreasonably withheld.

## 6. DUBBING/SUBTITLING RESTRICTIONS
- **6.1. Quality Standards:** All dubbing and subtitling must meet RHD's quality standards and be subject to RHD's approval.
- **6.2. Technical Accuracy:** All technical jargon and cultural nuances must be accurately localized.

## 9. EXCLUSIVITY OVERLAP
- **9.2 EXCLUSIVITY OVERLAP:** Several key titles in the Catalog (e.g., "The Berlin Cipher") have complex, overlapping exclusivity windows with other platforms. RHD represents and warrants that it has the right to grant the licenses specified in this Agreement, but Licensee acknowledges the potential for legal disputes in certain territories.

## 12. INDEMNITY & LIABILITY
- **12.1. Licensor's Representation:** RHD represents and warrants that it owns all necessary rights to the Catalog.
- **12.2. Licensee's Indemnity:** Licensee shall indemnify and hold RHD harmless from any claims arising from the Licensee's use or distribution of the Catalog.

## 14. MISCELLANEOUS
- **14.3 MATCHING RIGHTS:** For selected titles (e.g., "Red Harbor"), RHD has the right to match any third-party offer for spin-off, sequel, or prequel rights. 
- **14.5 CONDITIONAL APPROVAL:** The Licensee's right to exhibit certain titles in specific MENA and SEA regions is conditional upon the Licensee's successful completion of the cultural review and adaptation process, as specified in Section 3.1.

## 15. TERMINATION TRIGGERS
- **15.1. Non-Payment:** Failure to pay the license fee within 30 days of the due date.
- **15.2. Material Breach:** Any significant breach of the Grant of Rights or Exclusivity provisions.
- **15.3. Catalog Mix Change:** Removal of more than 5 key titles from the Catalog may trigger a termination review at the discretion of the Licensee.

## Conclusion
This contract for the Red Harbor Collection is a volume-focused and binding agreement. The exclusivity overlap, sub-licensing limitations, and cultural compliance provisions are of particular significance for the long-term success of the acquisition and the Licensee's platform.
