# Catalog Performance Summary: Red Harbor Collection

## Historical Performance Overview
- **Total Catalog Views (Estimated):** 100M+ across various third-party platforms in the last 2 years.
- **Average Completion Rate:** 65% (Steady for a mixed catalog).
- **Top Performing Genres:** Action/Thriller (75% completion), International Indie (60%), Documentary (55%).

## Regional Appeal Profile
- **Europe:** Very Strong (FR, DE, UK, ES). High demand for both indie and action.
- **Asia:** Strong (JP, KR, IN). Focus on neo-noir, martial arts, and documentaries.
- **Americas:** Moderate to Strong (US, CA, MX, BR). US/CA favor action; MX/BR favor thrillers and indie.
- **MENA/SEA:** Moderate. Several titles have high potential but require cultural review.

## Content Age Profile
- **Recent (2023-2024):** 38% of catalog. High demand, "fresher" feel.
- **Mid (2021-2022):** 42% of catalog. Solid performers with established fanbases.
- **Older (Pre-2021):** 20% of catalog. Library value, "long-tail" viewership.

## ROI Upside/Downside Analysis
- **Upside:** 
  - High volume of content for a single license fee.
  - Several "breakout" international titles (e.g., "Neon City Nights") can drive substantial adoption.
  - Strong "long-tail" potential for documentaries and indie films.
- **Downside:**
  - High localization costs for international titles.
  - Exclusivity overlap risks for several key titles.
  - Potential for lower-than-average completion rates for niche or older titles.

## Conclusion
The Red Harbor Collection is a high-performance content library with a strong global footprint. The mix of recent hits and established library titles provides a balanced ROI profile. By focusing on the top-performing genres and regions, the Platform can maximize the value of this acquisition and drive substantial audience engagement.
